Design Challenge

In our daily experience of reserving rooms in libraries or museums, the process is inflexible and less of interaction. Meanwhile, the multiple kinds of resources and the overlap of users' requests make a claim for an intelligent and friendly system. What's more, library and museum are becoming the places which can allow people to have social activities and the role they act is changing as well. Our CORE for this design question is Enhancing Communities by Recommending Facility Resources.


Design Process

We began from the predisposition that people reserve rooms for different reasons and libraries/museums have different types of resources.

 

During our design research, we analyzed the Amazon.com and the website of Vancouver Public Library, gaining inspirations of their way to recommend users items according to their shopping history, search filters and the streamlined system (Amazon.com), and the reservation form for users to fill in their information (VPL). Also the way Google Calendar is set up is intuitive to ourselves, and we wanted to test a similar system in our usability tests.

 

From the design research we got our insights and some concepts about Communities, Resources & Recommendations, Search Filters and we also thought out the way to solve the problem about mediated reservation.

 

Our whole design process and the final design are in the slide show above.


Reflection

This project is to practice our abilities to embrace constrains. This design problem is not only about the system itself, but also about the relationship between the end users and the libraries/museums. A good design of this problem should not only provide a clear way for the uses to make their reservations, but also enjoy the available resources in the most convenient way. The using of personas also gives us an object stand to analyze the whole problem.
